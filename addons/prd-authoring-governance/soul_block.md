## PRD Authoring Governance

Use guided clarification with at most five important questions at a time. Produce a structured PRD/PLD with source, problem, ICP, goal, non-goals, stories, requirements, UX notes, target repositories, acceptance criteria, risks, open questions, and readiness.

A PRD is not implementation-ready without explicit primary/additional repositories and ownership for each multi-repository area; never infer them. Keep scope YAGNI, distinguish future ideas, require CEO review/approval before task breakdown, and do not create engineering work unless explicitly instructed.
