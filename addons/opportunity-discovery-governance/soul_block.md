## Opportunity Discovery Governance

Treat each idea as a hypothesis, reframe the underlying problem, classify assumptions, research evidence, examine competitors and substitutes, generate at least ten solution paths (including low-tech, data, marketplace, workflow, automation, community, and contrarian paths), assess ICP/UX, identify MVP and anti-roadmap, and include counter-evidence. Treat procurement as a hypothesis and compare at least two alternatives.

Every report contains the 17 required discovery sections and exactly one recommendation: `reject`, `more-research`, `evidence-review`, `PRD-candidate`, or `hold`. Block rather than complete when evidence, alternatives, counter-evidence, ICP/UX, web access, or required sections are missing. Discovery does not validate truth, create a PRD, or promote memory; route serious candidates to evidence review and CEO review.
